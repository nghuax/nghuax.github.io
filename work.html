Binh An Coffee / brand + web.

RoleLead designer
Timeline5 weeks
ScopeIdentity, packaging, landing page
ToolsFigma, Illustrator, HTML/CSS

The brief was to make a local roaster feel premium without copying the usual minimalist coffee-brand formula.

I built the direction around ceramic warmth, quiet typography, and a red-gold palette that felt rooted rather than trendy. The web layer was treated like a short editorial issue: one dominant story, one conversion path, and a packaging system that could scale across seasonal releases.

  • A tightened tone-of-voice guide for product descriptions and seasonal drops.
  • Three packaging principles balancing shelf presence with production realism.
  • A launch page system with reusable modules for story, roast notes, and CTA blocks.

Delivered

  • 1 visual territory with packaging mockups and type rules.
  • 6 adaptable landing-page sections for future campaigns.
  • A handoff pack covering spacing, tokens, and editorial copy patterns.

Song Xanh Atlas / product + data.

RoleProduct designer
Timeline8 weeks
ScopeResearch synthesis, IA, UI
ToolsFigma, FigJam, Mapbox

The challenge was not making the atlas look smart. It was making dense environmental information feel readable to non-specialists.

I structured the product as a slow-reading experience with strong chapter breaks, steady typographic hierarchy, and an explicit relationship between map state and narrative state. Every interface choice was meant to reduce cognitive jumping between prose, evidence, and geography.

  • Reframed the information architecture around story chapters rather than datasets.
  • Designed comparison states for river health, intervention, and risk.
  • Specified annotation patterns so researchers could update evidence without rewriting layout logic.

What mattered

This project succeeded when the interface stopped competing with the report. The final system gave the team a shared grammar for maps, side notes, source citations, and long-form reading states.

Vuon Plant Care / mobile system.

RoleUI systems designer
Timeline6 weeks
ScopeMobile UI, components, flows
ToolsFigma, Protopie

Plant-care apps often overdesign encouragement. I wanted the product to feel more like a steady companion than a gamified feed.

The interface language used soft constraints: clear status colors, simple care tasks, and a calm motion system built around confirmation rather than reward. I prioritized components that could stretch from onboarding to reminders without splitting into separate visual dialects.

  • A compact component library for cards, reminders, chips, and check-ins.
  • Notification patterns tuned for recurrence without feeling punitive.
  • Illustration guidance that supported recognition without turning decorative.

System principles

  • One state language across home, detail, and reminders.
  • Small, legible action clusters for one-handed use.
  • Motion only where it improved reassurance or confirmation.

Nha Magazine / editorial translation.

RoleEditorial designer
Timeline4 weeks
ScopeWeb adaptation, grids, type
ToolsInDesign, Figma, CSS

The print edition already had a voice. The digital task was to preserve that voice without turning the site into a PDF with a scrollbar.

I translated the magazine's dense typography into a more breathable web system with rhythm changes at essay breaks, larger pull-quote moments, and image treatments that held atmosphere without collapsing performance.

  • Responsive editorial grids tuned for essays, interviews, and image-led features.
  • A hierarchy model separating issue identity from article identity.
  • Reusable typography specs for Vietnamese and English publishing.

Output

The team left with a translation framework, not a one-off mockup: page templates, spacing rules, and a set of publishing decisions they could repeat.
