Portfolio Entry 4 / Trend Analysis

Social commerce · An Thai

Trend Analysis.

—

Portfolio Entry 4 — Trend analysis.

This final portfolio entry examines a current digital marketing trend and evaluates how An Thai could realistically take advantage of it. The trend selected is social commerce — chosen not because it is fashionable, but because it fits both the Vietnamese market context and An Thai's current digital gap. Social commerce is the trend in which An Thai's existing credibility assets can most directly produce commercial outcomes, given the scale of social platform use in Vietnam and the current weakness of the brand's website as a pure conversion channel.

01

The trend of social commerce.

Social commerce describes the integration of content, community and transaction inside social media platforms. Rather than treating social as a top-of-funnel awareness channel and the website as the place where sales happen, social commerce collapses discovery, consideration and purchase into a single connected surface (Solomon & Tuten, 2018; HubSpot, 2024).

Two pieces of Vietnamese market context make this trend particularly relevant. First, VECOM (2025) estimates that Vietnam's e-commerce market reached US$32 billion in 2024, with 27% growth, and online retail sales reached US$22.5 billion, growing 30%. Second, Vietnam has one of the largest social-media populations in Southeast Asia — 76.2 million Facebook users, 55.9 million Messenger users, and a rapidly growing TikTok audience (DataReportal, 2025a; 2025b; 2025c). Together, these conditions make social commerce a commercially realistic trend rather than a speculative one.

02

The way brands can take advantage of the trend.

Brands that make social commerce work do not simply “post more”. They design a connected sequence in which each stage of the customer journey lives on a social surface. In practical terms, this sequence is usually described as a loop: discover → ask → buy → return. Social posts create discovery; Messenger or comments support enquiry; product pages or marketplace listings support evaluation and purchase; and follow-up messaging supports repeat buying (Chaffey & Smith, 2023; Solomon & Tuten, 2018).

A second practical theme is focus over breadth. Successful social commerce programmes typically launch with a small number of product lines and a small set of repeatable content themes, then widen once data accumulates. HubSpot (2024) notes that social media marketers in 2024 increasingly prioritise repeatable content formats over one-off campaigns, both to control resource demand and to build recognisable brand cues over time.

Two specific application areas illustrate how these principles translate to An Thai: (1) social discovery and content-driven product storytelling, and (2) Messenger-led commerce.

03

Social commerce in
discovery-led product storytelling.

The first application area is social discovery — the use of short-form social content on Facebook and TikTok to introduce, contextualise and sell products inside the feed. Coffee is well suited to this application because it is visually and culturally rich: origin, brewing ritual, café lifestyle, and gifting are all content genres that travel well on social platforms. Mayrhofer et al. (2019) further show that brand content presented by peers (rather than by the brand) raises purchase intent among younger audiences, which reinforces the commercial case for discovery-led content.

De Veirman, Cauberghe and Hudders (2017) and Freberg et al. (2011) similarly demonstrate that creator-led content on social platforms exerts a measurable effect on brand attitude and perceived authenticity. The implication is that a small set of recurring collaborations — with Vietnamese baristas, home brewers or food creators — can efficiently populate the discovery surface without the brand having to produce everything itself.

An Thai and the discovery surface

For An Thai, the credibility inputs required for discovery-led storytelling already exist. The origin story (Vietnamese coffee heritage since 1996), the product range (instant, freeze-dried, 3-in-1, extracted liquid) and the certification stack (ISO, HACCP, Halal, BRC, Kosher, SNI) are documented on the corporate site (An Thai Group, n.d.). The Vua Chồn (King Weasel) line on the consumer site provides a ready-made premium anchor for creator-led content (AnTháiCafé, n.d.).

A realistic 90-day pilot for An Thai would nominate two product lines — one mass-market (3-in-1 mix) and one premium (Vua Chồn or freeze-dried) — and three content themes: origin, brewing ritual and gifting. Each theme would run for roughly thirty days, allowing engagement data to accumulate before pivoting. This is the same focus-over-breadth principle identified in the literature.

04

Social commerce in
Messenger-led commerce.

The second application area is conversational, Messenger-led commerce. In Vietnam, Messenger is not a secondary surface: its 55.9 million users (DataReportal, 2025b) make it a mainstream commercial channel that consumers already use to ask about prices, availability, delivery and product suitability. For a brand whose current website hides consumer prices behind “Liên hệ”, Messenger is effectively already functioning as the de-facto product page.

The opportunity is to formalise this behaviour rather than leave it implicit. Baird and Parasnis (2011) describe this shift in broader terms as the move from social media to social customer relationship management: brands turn inbound conversations into managed, repeatable service processes. Practically, this means documented Messenger response times, a small set of templated replies for the most common product questions, and a clearly defined escalation path when an enquiry becomes a purchase intent.

The commercial prize of Messenger-led commerce is retention. Because coffee is a repeat-purchase category, a user who has bought once through a Messenger conversation is a strong candidate for a reorder prompt three weeks later. Vivek, Beatty and Morgan (2012) argue that customer engagement — defined to include behaviour beyond the initial transaction — is a distinct and measurable construct; retention messaging is one of the most direct ways to act on it.

An Thai and the Messenger surface

For An Thai, Messenger-led commerce requires a response playbook rather than a technology change. The components are modest: a first-response SLA of under four working hours, one FAQ document covering product range and delivery, three saved replies for the most common questions, and a retention message three weeks after purchase with a reorder link. This scope is achievable for the size of business An Thai represents (51–200 employees, according to its LinkedIn profile) without hiring a dedicated team.

Combined with the discovery application in Section 3, the two applications form a connected loop: social content creates discovery, Messenger supports the ask and purchase stages, and retention messaging closes the loop back to repeat discovery. This is the structure in which An Thai's existing trust assets begin to behave as a commercial system.

05

Opportunities & risks for An Thai.

Social commerce is commercially attractive for An Thai, but it is not risk-free. The opportunities concentrate around the brand's existing credibility assets; the risks concentrate around operational readiness and landing-page clarity. The table below summarises the opportunity–risk matrix.

Social commerce is commercially attractive for An Thai — but only if operational response and landing-page clarity improve.

The overall conclusion of this portfolio entry is that social commerce is the most realistic digital growth path for An Thai. It does not require a rebrand or a site rebuild. It asks only that the brand's existing content assets (origin, product range, certifications) and existing channels (Facebook, Messenger, product pages) be connected into a single repeatable loop. In a Vietnamese market projected to continue growing at thirty per cent a year in online retail (VECOM, 2025), that loop is where An Thai's credibility is most efficiently converted into commercial outcomes.
